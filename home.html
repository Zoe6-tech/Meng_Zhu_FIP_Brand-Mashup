We   Are   From   Polish


Tyskie-name comes from the brewery located in the Upper Silesia town of Tychy.The brand is brewed by the Princely Brewery Tychy (Polish Tyskie Browary Ksiazece), part of the Kompania Piwowarska brewery group, a former subsidiary of SABMiller since 1999 as a result of the merger of two SABMiller owned Polish breweries, the Lech Browary Wielkopolski (Lech Brewery Greater Poland) in Poznań, and the Tyskie Browary Ksiazece in Tychy.

The award-winning Polish beer Tyskie, one of the most popular beers in Poland.Tyskie has all the typical characteristics of a pale lager — yellow color, medium carbonation, slight bready malt and grassy hops in the aroma, which carries over into the taste. It produced a white fluffy head that sunk fairly quickly.

Sweeter Tyskie differs from other lagers.In a other word, the main difference between Tyskie and others beers is that it is sweeter.The sweetness is nothing terrible, just different.The sweetness in the 5.3% ABV Tyskie is undercut by some bitterness at the end, which helps balance out the lager.
